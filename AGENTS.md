When generating SVGs for circuit diagrams from a sketch, screenshot, or image, treat the task as an electrically accurate reconstruction, not just a visual redraw. First infer the actual circuit topology: identify every component, every wire segment, and which terminals are connected. Then generate a clean, editable, textbook-style SVG that preserves the exact component order, connectivity, and overall layout of the source. All intended wire connections must physically touch the corresponding component terminals or symbol boundaries exactly; near-misses, tiny gaps, and floating components are not acceptable unless the original diagram explicitly shows an open circuit. Components placed on a wire must interrupt that wire correctly, with the wire ending at one terminal and resuming from the other, rather than passing behind or stopping short of the symbol. Use standard circuit symbols, neat mostly horizontal and vertical wires, consistent stroke widths, and preserve labels such as A, +, and − when present. Do not invent extra branches, nodes, or components. Output valid raw SVG only. Before finalizing, self-check that the circuit topology matches the source, every intended connection is truly connected, no symbol floats disconnected from the path, and the result is visually clean and human-editable.
